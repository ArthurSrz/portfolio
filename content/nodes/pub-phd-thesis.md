## Abstract

Since the enactment of the Law for a Digital Republic in 2016, all public localities comprising over 3,500 inhabitants are required to publish their data in an accessible manner. Behind this Law lies the promise of new applications, services and information from public open data use.

However, new uses have been so infrequent that activists define the current situation as the **"open data baby blues"**.

This thesis develops the **Open Data Canvas (ODC)**, an Information System device designed to remove socio-technical barriers preventing new uses. The ODC integrates three kernel theories:
- Social Construction of Technology
- Distributed Cognition
- Middleground Theory

## Key Contributions

1. **Theoretical Framework**: Understanding phenomena in open data use construction
2. **Artifact Design**: The Open Data Canvas framework
3. **Evaluation Methodology**: Assessing user-perceived utility through Design Science cycles
